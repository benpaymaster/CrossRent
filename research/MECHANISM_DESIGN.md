# CrossRent: Cryptoeconomic Mechanism Design

## Abstract
In high-throughput execution environments (Monad/Arc), the primary threat to protocol liveness is not isolated hardware failure, but **Correlated Faults**. This document specifies a Quadratic Slashing mechanism designed to maximize the **Cost to Disrupt (CtD)**.

## 1. The Correlated Penalty Function
We define the penalty $P$ for an individual node $i$ failing within a window of $t$ slots as a function of the total number of faulty nodes $f$:

$$P_i(f) = s_i \cdot \left( \frac{f}{N} \right)^2$$

Where:
* $s_i$ is the individual stake.
* $N$ is the total active validator set.
* $f$ is the count of nodes reporting conflicting state transitions.

## 2. Sybil Resistance Analysis
Under a linear slashing model, an attacker with $33\%$ of the stake loses exactly $33\%$ of their capital to trigger a liveness failure. Under CrossRent's quadratic model, the penalty for a $33\%$ attack scales exponentially:

$$TotalLoss = \sum_{i=1}^{f} P_i(f) = f \cdot s_i \cdot \left( \frac{f}{N} \right)^2$$

This creates a **Super-linear Security Margin**, ensuring that as an attacker’s influence grows, their risk exposure grows at $O(f^3)$.
